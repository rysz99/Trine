Locations

" The Trine " game has many cleverly made locations, through which we move the main characters. It is visible that their magnificence lies mainly in the originality of the creators, rather than in modeling themselves on the beauty of nature. This is a confirmation of the intention regarding the plot of the entire game, which tells about the corruption of nature and magic by Evil, which reigned in the Kingdom as a result of the theft of two Artifacts, which disturbed the harmony. Controlling a thief, knight or wizard, we get to places such as:

Astral Academy - the place where it all began. It was here that the Trine summoned the three heroes, entrusting them with the mission of saving the Kingdom from darkness and decay.

Academy Hallways is the next level of our game. This is the level where all the fun really begins, because the previous one was just a warm-up, a tutorial.

Wolvercote Catacombs - here for the first time we have to deal with skeleton archers, who hit us from a distance while being attacked by numerous skeletons equipped with swords. Before moving on to the next level we face a big mechanical skeleton and moving guillotines. The knight gains the Titan Power.

Dragon Graveyard this is the level where bats start to make our lives miserable, agile, flying and hurting. The wizard claims that this is the fastest way to the Tower of Sarek, or the Tower of Evil, as some call it.

Crystal Caverns - enemies appear, breathing fire from torches at us. For the first time, the heroes' resistance to being submerged under water is tested.

Crypt of the Damned is a location in the game that starts off quite dangerously, as we are dealing with moving, giant guillotines for the first time.

Forsaken Dangeons - Mutant spiders join the already large number of dangerous enemies. They shoot webs at us or bite us when we get close enough.

Throne of the Lost King- our heroes are searching for the second Artifact that protected the Castle and the King.

Fangle Forest The three heroes, the Knight, the Thief and the Wizard must now travel through Fangle Forest to continue on their quest.

Shadowthorn Thicket- this is the tenth level. Shadowthorn Thicket is the only way to the ancient ruins named The Ruins of The Perished, meant in the ancient scripts read by the Wizard.

Ruins of the Perished- this level is distinguished not only by the above-average beauty of the surroundings, such as impressive ruins bathed in the rays of the sun at full strength, but also by the multitude of hanging or floating bridges and the skeletons with arches lurking there. In addition, we spend most of our time underwater looking for experience points or energy.

Heartland Mines- this is a level that requires the agility of the Thief due to the numerous moving obstacles in the form of carts, rungs suspended high on the ceiling, ramps. The Wizard and the ability he gains - the Levitating Platform - are also invaluable, because we sometimes move forward without any support that Zoya could use.

Bramblestoke Village it's a place abandoned by its inhabitants, taken over by an army of the undead. At first, our heroes wanted to save whoever they could, but after a while they knew that the only people they had to save were themselves üòê

Iron Forge is the place where the undead forge their deadly weapons. Pontius the Knight immediately recognized the smell of weapons being forged, but in his opinion, he has the best weapon.

Tower of Sarek This is the last level of the game, where we first have to face Sarek's ambushes, including lava rising at a dizzying pace or obstacles conjured by him. After dealing with the starting package of death, the premium version awaits us - whole hordes of skeletons gaping and chopping and bats, constantly bothering us with their attacks from the sky. And Sarek, whenever we clear our way to the very top, where we separate the souls of our heroes and return all the Artifacts, sends us more obstacles. The game ends when, despite everything, we manage, and the king of Evil and monsters gets hit in the head with a knight's hammer üòÉ
